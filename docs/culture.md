## Arts & Culture

### National Broadcaster
**Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)** — Australia's national public broadcaster, funded by the federal government but editorially independent.
- Television, radio, and digital news/content
- **URL:** [abc.net.au](https://www.abc.net.au)

**Special Broadcasting Service (SBS)** — Multilingual and multicultural public broadcaster.
- **URL:** [sbs.com.au](https://www.sbs.com.au)

### Film
- **Screen Australia** — national arts agency supporting Australian screen content
- **URL:** [screenaustralia.gov.au](https://www.screenaustralia.gov.au)
- Notable filmmakers: Baz Luhrmann, Peter Weir, Jane Campion (NZ/AU)
- Notable actors: Cate Blanchett, Hugh Jackman, Nicole Kidman, Chris Hemsworth

### Literature
- **Australian literature prize:** Miles Franklin Literary Award (est. 1957)
- Notable authors: Tim Winton, Richard Flanagan, Kate Grenville, Peter Carey

### Australian Open (Tennis)
- First Grand Slam of the year
- Held annually in January, Melbourne Park
- One of four tennis Grand Slam tournaments
- **URL:** [australianopen.com](https://www.australianopen.com)

### Olympic Record
Australia has competed at every Summer Olympic Games held since 1896. Hosted in **1956 (Melbourne)** and **2000 (Sydney)**.
- **Committee:** [olympics.com.au](https://www.olympics.com.au)
- **2032 host:** Brisbane, QLD
