## Overview

Australia is consistently ranked among the world's top tourist destinations. In 2024, international arrivals approached pre-pandemic levels. Domestic tourism also represents a significant part of the economy.

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| International visitors (2024 est.) | ~9.5 million |
| Top source markets | New Zealand, USA, UK, China, India |
| Total tourism spend (2024 est.) | ~AUD $170 billion (domestic + international) |
| National tourism body | Tourism Australia |

## Entry Requirements

### Visas
Most international visitors require a visa or travel authority before entering Australia. The main options are:

| Visa/Authority | Eligible countries | Link |
|---|---|---|
| eVisitor (subclass 651) | EU, select European countries | [immi.homeaffairs.gov.au](https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-finder) |
| Electronic Travel Authority — ETA (subclass 601) | USA, UK, Canada, Japan, Singapore, and others | [eta.homeaffairs.gov.au](https://www.homeaffairs.gov.au/trav/visi/eta) |
| Visitor visa (subclass 600) | All nationalities | [immi.homeaffairs.gov.au](https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/visitor-600) |
| New Zealand citizens | No visa required; Special Category visa on arrival | — |

**Visa check tool:** [homeaffairs.gov.au/trav/visi](https://www.homeaffairs.gov.au/trav/visi)

### Border Biosecurity
Australia has extremely strict biosecurity controls. Failure to declare food, plant material, or animal products can result in significant fines.
- **Authority:** Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (DAFF)
- **Declaration:** [agriculture.gov.au/biosecurity](https://www.agriculture.gov.au/biosecurity-trade/import/cant-bring-in)

## Travel Essentials

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Driving | **Left-hand traffic**; International Driving Permit accepted |
| Currency | AUD; credit cards widely accepted; ATMs/eftpos abundant |
| Electricity | 230V / 50Hz; Type I plug (two or three flat pins) |
| Time zones | AEDT (UTC+11), AEST (UTC+10), ACST (UTC+9:30), AWST (UTC+8) |
| Water | Tap water is safe to drink in all major cities |
| Tipping | Not customary; optional in restaurants |
| Internet | Good 4G/5G coverage in cities; limited in remote areas |
| SIM cards | Available at airports and major retailers (Telstra, Optus, Vodafone) |

## Travel Advisories

The Australian Government's Smartraveller service provides safety and travel advisories for Australians overseas. For visiting travellers, check your own government's travel advisory for Australia.

- **Smartraveller (for Australians abroad):** [smartraveller.gov.au](https://www.smartraveller.gov.au)
