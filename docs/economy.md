## Overview

Australia has a highly developed mixed-market economy. It has experienced over 30 consecutive years of GDP growth prior to COVID-19, one of the longest uninterrupted growth streaks of any advanced economy.

| Indicator | Value (2024 est.) |
|---|---|
| GDP (nominal) | ~USD $1.77 trillion |
| GDP per capita | ~USD $64,000 |
| Unemployment rate | ~4.0% |
| Inflation (CPI) | ~3.4% (RBA target band: 2–3%) |
| Current account | Surplus (driven by commodity exports) |
| Currency | Australian Dollar (AUD) |
| Sovereign rating | AAA (S&P, Fitch, Moody's) |

## Key Sectors

### Mining & Resources
Australia is the world's largest exporter of iron ore and among the top exporters of coal, LNG, and lithium. The Pilbara region (WA) drives much of the iron ore output.
- **Regulator:** Department of Industry, Science and Resources
- **Data:** [industry.gov.au/resources](https://www.industry.gov.au/sectors/mining-resources-and-energy)

### Agriculture
Wool, beef, wheat, barley, canola, dairy, and wine are major agricultural exports. Australia is one of the few food-surplus nations.
- **Regulator:** Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry
- **Data:** [agriculture.gov.au](https://www.agriculture.gov.au)

### Financial Services
Sydney is Australia's financial capital. The "Big Four" banks (CBA, ANZ, NAB, Westpac) dominate. The superannuation system holds ~AUD $3.5 trillion in assets.
- **Regulator:** Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA)
- **Securities regulator:** Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)

### Education (Export)
International education is one of Australia's largest service exports (~AUD $36 billion annually). Administered under the ESOS Act.

### Healthcare & Life Sciences
A significant GDP contributor and major employer sector.
